About ASITFP

Ashoka Society for International Trade and Foreign Policy serves as a student-friendly forum of understanding economic foreign trade. Through the development of a youth centric perspective of how international trade operates, we aim to understand the complexities that are associated with international trade by putting ourselves in the shoes of diplomats that carry out these decisions and generating comprehensive research work that not only gives us knowledge about economic hard power of nations but also questions who really gains from trade unions, ‘fair’ trade treaties and other such agreements.

Founded by Ananya Agarwal and Lavanya Rathi, and under the guidance of our esteemed formal advisors Professor Abhishek Rai and Professor Nayan Chanda and informal advisors Professor Quintijn Kat, Professor Amit Julka and Professor Shivshankar Menon the society will conduct simulations of the WTO, much like an MUN. This activity will act as the World Trade Organisation where students will behave as representatives of nations discussing matters of trade and commerce. An e-newspaper in the form of a Foreign Policy Review will keep the student body updated on matters related to international economics and trade via weekly articles. Student writers may take up topics such as new economic disputes, developments or amendments in international law related to trade. It will provide updates on the happenings of the world of international commerce.

We encourage those with a keen interest in world affairs, foreign policy, and economic reforms to join. Whether you’re a seasoned student activist, or a newcomer looking for a place to start, our society has something in store for everyone!

Our Advisors

Abhishek Rai

Assistant Professor of Economics, Ashoka University

Abhishek Rai completed his PhD in Economics from Pennsylvania State University. His primary research interests are in International Trade and Spatial Economics. Dr Rai’s current research explores the welfare and productivity implications of mobility of people (migration) and goods (trade) between locations using quantitative models which emphasize general equilibrium forces. He uses administrative micro-data to motivate, quantify, and validate these models. Dr Rai has an MA in Economics from Delhi School of Economics and BTech from Vellore Institute of Technology.

Nayan Chanda

Associate Professor of International Relations, Ashoka University.

Nayan Chanda, an associate professor of international relations, has a rich history in journalism and academia. He served as the bureau chief of Far Eastern Economic Review in wartime Saigon, later becoming the magazine's editor - the first Asian in that role. At Yale University's Center for the Study of Globalization, he directed publications and established YaleGlobal Online. Nayan Chanda's interests span Asian history and globalization, reflected in his authored books like "Bound Together" (2007) and "Brother Enemy" (1986), as well as numerous co-authored works. He remains an active contributor to international newspapers with articles and reviews.
