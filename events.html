Inaugural Event, ASITFP

The Ashoka Society for International Trade and Foreign Policy (ASITFP) held its inaugural event on [Event Date] at [Event Venue]. The event was a significant milestone in launching ASITFP's activities and providing a platform for students interested in international relations and diplomacy.

Ambassador Mohan Kumar, a distinguished diplomat with 36 years of service in the Indian Foreign Services, graced the event as the Guest of Honor. His illustrious career includes notable roles such as India's lead negotiator at the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT) and the World Trade Organization (WTO). He is also the Chairman of the Research and Information System for Developing Countries (RIS) and has served as Joint Secretary at the Ministry of External Affairs.

The event commenced with the formal introduction of ASITFP, followed by an enlightening training bootcamp led by Ambassador Kumar. This training aimed to equip participants with essential skills in the fields of foreign policy and diplomacy. The bootcamp was a valuable opportunity for attendees to learn from Ambassador Kumar's extensive expertise.

A luncheon, thoughtfully organized by ASITFP, facilitated informal interactions between Ambassador Kumar and the attendees. This allowed participants to gain personal insights and engage in discussions about various aspects of international relations.

The inaugural event held special significance as an integral part of ASITFP's induction program. The training bootcamp, led by Ambassador Kumar, offered participants a unique chance to learn practical skills and gain insights into the intricate world of diplomacy. It was particularly beneficial for those interested in International Relations and International Economics.

To ensure the event's success and relevance, ASITFP conducted a survey to gauge student interest prior to extending an official invitation to Ambassador Kumar. The valuable feedback received will inform future engagements and events. Attendees were also informed about upcoming inductions, which will take place following this event and shortly before the next.

The inaugural event of ASITFP marked the beginning of an exciting journey towards fostering a deeper understanding of international trade and foreign policy. Ambassador Mohan Kumar's presence and the active engagement of participants highlighted the enthusiasm for learning and exploring the realms of diplomacy and global relations.





PolicyCraft, ASITFP

Event Report: The PoliCraft: Trade Governance Innovation Challenge

Date: August 26th - 27th, 2023

Location: Ashoka University, India (Virtual and On-site)

In August 2023, The PoliCraft: Trade Governance Innovation Challenge, presented by ASITFP in partnership with The Geostrata, Geneva Trade Platform, and CUTS Geneva, unfolded at the picturesque Ashoka University campus in India. This highly anticipated two-day event drew together global applicants, accomplished mentors, and distinguished trade policy experts to explore the complexities of trade governance.

Key Highlights:

  • Policy Exploration: The event's core focus centered on a deep dive into policy analysis and proposal submissions. Participants, representing diverse backgrounds and perspectives, showcased their dedication to crafting visionary, sustainable, and pragmatic trade policies capable of addressing contemporary global challenges.
  • Expert Insights: Renowned mentors and seasoned trade policy experts, often at the forefront of global trade negotiations, generously shared their wealth of knowledge and expertise. Their insights offered participants invaluable perspectives on the real-world dynamics of international trade.
  • Networking: Beyond the formal sessions, the event facilitated a vibrant atmosphere for networking and collaboration. Attendees seized the opportunity to forge meaningful connections with peers, experts, and like-minded policy enthusiasts from around the world.

The PoliCraft: Trade Governance Innovation Challenge was an unqualified success, embodying its mission to empower the next generation of policy-makers. By fostering an environment where fresh ideas met practical experience, the event contributed significantly to the discourse on progressive trade policies.

Participants left with enhanced skills, a broader network, and a deeper appreciation for the complexities of global trade governance. This event was not merely a gathering; it was a catalyst for future leaders in the realm of international policy-making.

Ashoka Society for International Trade and Foreign Policy serves as a student-friendly forum of understanding economic foreign trade. Founded by Ananya Agarwal and Lavanya Rathi, and under the guidance of our esteemed formal advisors Professor Abhishek Rai and Professor Nayan Chanda and informal advisors Professor Quintijn Kat, Professor Amit Julka and Professor Shivshankar Menon the society will conduct simulations of the WTO, much like an MUN.
